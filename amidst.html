Amidst

Overview
Motion poetry for the unseen among us
Audience
Poety fans
Keywords
Motion Graphics
Poetry
Second Year

Problem

Poetry is often seen as something that is accessible by a very small handful of individuals. Conversely, video and motion often lack the depth to make them lasting and memorable.

To combine these two art forms has the potential of highlighting the best of each, in order to create a transcendental hybrid.

Brief

The aim of the brief was to eloquently combine two powerful mediums of storytelling. The first half is the Haiku, a type of Japanese short-form poetry that aims to tell a short story in three lines. The second half is a motion graphic, a form of video that focuses on moving elements created for a specific purpose or theme.

These two forces are then combined to form a single whole. Furthermore, three distinct Haiku motion graphics needed to work together towards a singular narrative spread across them.

Solution

Amidst is a series of moving poetry pieces that abstract the journey of life. Centered around an unnamed character of black cloth, it tells the story of the beginning of life’s journey through to the end we have to come to terms with life and by extension, ourselves.

The motion graphics are meant to be watched in order, to experience the building narrative as it unfolds across the one-hundred and twenty second runtime. The viewer is invited to develop their own interpretation of what they see.

Backstory

I love writing poems, and Haikus are a perfect format for writing poems as they are short, concise and to the point. This brief was both challenging and fun, as it helped me to learn Cinema 4D better, but also express myself in a new medium. I liked the idea of having a character around which the experience centered, which may have been in part due to the animation we did at the beginning of our second year. I worked with the concept that each of the three motion graphics would build on one another, in order to tell a miniature story across them.

While the whole narrative is unpacked in the way I see it, below, it was very much designed such that the viewer is also able to create their own interpretations of the story. The cloth represents the viewer as much as it is a character in the narrative, and therefore is a blank canvas upon which the viewer may impart their own meaning. The story revolves around life as an experience, and no one will have the same experience.

Outset

(noun)   the start or beginning of something.
The first motion graphic delves into how the cloth is moving through its journey. The cloth is a representation of the unseen and unheard. The first stanza tells of how the cloth, and perhaps other like it, has travelled from somewhere. We are not sure why they are travelling yet, but the ice field gives a sense of scale of where the cloth came from, and where it is going.

The second section talks about how the cloth is travelling to meet the unknown, represented in the diamond floating in a empty landscape. The moving shadows show a passing of time, implying that the unknown exists beyond the comprehension of time and place. The final section brings us back to cloth to show that it is still travelling from afar, it’s journey far from over.


Median

(adjective)   denoting the middle term (or mean of the middle two terms) of a series arranged in order of magnitude.
The second motion graphic deals primarily with scale and movement, as a continuation of the cloth’s journey. The Haiku was written to project a sense of contrast between the cloth and the surroundings. It is only able to go with what its environment will it to, simply flapping in the breeze, otherwise unmoving. The terrain is stark, to further impress the sensation of desolation.

The multiple pillars in the terrain imply that the cloth used to not be alone, but was once accompanied by others, now long since gone. The height of the pillars shows that escaping from this cycle is virtually impossible, and how detached being on one of these pillars is from the rest of the world.

The ‘shoulders of giants’ implies that this path has followed by others before, that have made it through it. The cloth has thus also entered into a new realm that will separate it from its contemporaries, because it has made it further into the journey than anyone else. There is no turning back; the start of the journey is further away than the end.


Culmination

(noun)    the highest or climactic point of something, especially as attained after a long time.
The third motion graphic represents the final leg of the journey. The cloth has made it to the highest pillar, shown by the thick mist enveloping everything else around. Having made it through the journey, it now waits patiently, floating on the pillars of eternity. The second section shows how the world below is as it was before, empty and unchanged, as though no one has noticed the journey that it has undertaken.

In the final section, which shows what the journey was all for, is a representation of transcendence, finding divinity. The cloth had stared into the abyss of its life and the abyss stares back, in a gratifying notion of acknowledgement.

Ultimately the three motion graphics tell the story of how the journey of one’s life to whatever our ultimate goal may be, is one that needs to be walked alone, and done for no one but oneself.
