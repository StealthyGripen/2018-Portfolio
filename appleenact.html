Apple Enact

Overview
Accesibilty made accesible
Audience
Sign language speakers, both hearing and hearing impaired
Keywords
Digital Campaign
App Design
UI/UX Design

Problem

There are 466 million people with disabling hearing loss in the world. Apple Music does not offer a way for hearing impaired music listeners to use the application. The music on the platform is audio-only, and does not allow for any services such as subtitles or external peripherals that could solve this. This leaves a large section of the market excluded.

Brief

Design an application that is able to tie in with Apple Music that allows it to extend its service to the hard-of-hearing, to be able to make use of the platform alongside hearing users.

Solution

There are roughly 70 million users of Sign Language in the United States, with a small number who actively create American Sign Language (ASL) interpretations of popular music. These are usually made by those with hearing, and are a very popular format for the hearing impaired to experience music. To harness this wealth of potential users, the Apple Enact app provides a platform to connect these interpreters and speakers through music.

The app uses the concept of community collaboration to encourage the creation of sign language interpretations of the music in the Apple Music catalogue. The addition of advanced AI and machine learning, make the package a complete solution for the hard-of-hearing to listen to their favourite music.

Interpret

The entire Enact app is based around the Interpret section, which serves as a hub for users to find and view interpretations. Upon opening the app, users will be greeted with the Popular page, which is curated selection of the most viewed and liked interpretations. These will often be made for songs that are popular and trending at the time. It will also feature content by well known interpreters and other music artists that have made their music videos sign-language friendly.

The Creators section is oriented towards community-made content, and will feature the top content creators or those that a user has subscribed to. This is where the core of the app comes to life, because anyone can get their interpretations featured, and gain a loyal following, further strengthening the sense of community.

Create +

This section of the app ties in closely with the functionality of the Interpret section. Users who are able to speak sign language are encouraged to interpret their favourite songs. The user picks a song that they would like to cover, and are presented with footage from their front camera to ensure correct framing. When record is tapped, scrolling lyrics are shown onscreen as well as the audio of the song.

This can be tagged and uploaded to the database.Their movement is tracked, and fed into the Enact AI to power its machine learning.

Library

The Library is the core of the app. All of the user’s favorite music, from downloaded tracks to bought songs appear here. Scrolling through the artists is aided by a simple alphabetical popup.

Tapping on an artist opens their page. From here the user can chose to listen to their popular songs, or go directly to a specific song.

When a song is chosen to be played, the user is taken to the player screen, and the most popular interpretation of the songs is automatically loaded and played.

Search

The Search screen is one the most powerful tools in the Enact app. It allows users to find anything within the app, from songs and albums to interpretations. It is available in every screen of the app as a small widget that can be dragged upwards to open the Search screen.

It makes use of powerful search suggestions to help the user find what they are looking for even faster. As they type, suggestions will fill the search field, with a longer, scrollable list appearing if the user stops typing for a moment. They are able to swipe sideways to switch between the search filters. Once they find what they are looking for, they can hit the search button again, and will be directed to the correct item or page.

Tactile

The Enact app functions as a way for the deaf and hard-of-hearing to see a representation of music. This leaves out a very important sensation, namely touch. The Apple Tactile steps in to fill this gap.

The device is a phone-sized slab, with a soft touch rubbery surface on top. The user places their fingers in the ambidextrous grooves, and connects the device to the Enact app.

The music file is processed in the cloud beforehand, or on the phone’s AI chip. The waveform is split into four distinct frequency bands, from the deepest bass to the highest treble. The user can then feel these vibrations through their fingers in real-time. The fingers are especially sensitive to vibration, meaning the experience is truly intense.

This can be purchased as an add-on to the Enact app, and is fully compatible with the app. It supports wireless charging.
