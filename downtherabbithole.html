Down the Rabbit Hole

Overview
A unique series of motion graphics for a unique music festival
Audience
Festival goers
Keywords
Motion Graphics
Branding

Problem

The Down the Rabbit Hole music festival needs to reinvent their visual language for the 2018 event, that is more modern and minimal, but without losing the brand’s essence.

They need to move away from the simple craft style of previous designs, and move towards the future. The design language needs to be time and place agnostic, in order to appeal to any potential attendees.

Brief

Develop a new visual brand for the Down the Rabbit Hole 2018 music festival, and a series of motion graphics to augment the design and promote the event. The new brand should speak to the core of the festival and reflect its uniqueness in an over-saturated world.

The motion graphics need to showcase some of the performing artists, each in a unique way, that reflects a particular aspect of the festival’s quintessential lifeblood. Coherency and eccentricity should be fused to create a professional yet unconventional set of branding.

Solution

Down the Rabbit Hole is something different to normal life - a home away from home where everyone is welcome. Down the Rabbit Hole is another strange and different world. A world that is weird and removed from ours. A world of beautiful contrast and unity.

You are invited to get lost in the following promotional content, and be transported to a world of simplicity and freedom. Harsh colours contrast soft and warm shapes, and jarring sounds give way to graceful movements. Everything is interwoven to represent the juxtaposition of the two lives - the one you leave at the entrance of the festival, and the one you get to live inside.

Promo

The promo video, makes strong use of matching the motion to the music, to create a perfectly balanced hybrid. It takes three different spheres and applies a modifier to them. One morphs into three clones of iself, as the different personalities of a person make reveal themselves. In the second, slices ripple through the shape as though sound waves moving through the body. The third bursts apart and comes back together intermittently, as though all inhibitions are ripped off to reveal something within. The ink cloud represents a sphere that has fully rendered itself, and allows itself to be shaped to whichever is required of it.

Logo Build

Tying into the prior themes of the past branding, the logo build uses the concept of a cloth covered ball to reveal the logo. The cloth starts out as a flat surface, that is then disturbed as the sphere moves it upwards, and eventually sheds the cloth to reveal the festival’s logo.

Not only does it tie in with past branding, but it also shows how the attendees are all something better beneath the cloth beneath which they hide in their lives outside of the festival.

Bumper 1

This bumper shows a large sphere passing by a smaller one, affecting its trajectory as they pass. This reflects how the festival moves through an attendee, and leaves them altered afterwards. They cannot leave the same person as they entered.

It highlights the artist Nils Frahm.

Bumper 2

This bumper shows small balls bouncing rhythmically, but slightly losing direction with each consecutive bounce. They are ultimately knocked off their path by a much larger sphere. This reflects how each festival attendee lives their lives outside the festival, trying to hold it together, and how the festival breaks down all those barriers and leaves everyone exposed.

It highlights the singer Fever Ray.

Bumper 3

This bumper is based upon planets with their moons orbiting them. This links to how people from all over, with vastly different backgrounds can come together and orbit around the festival as a central constant, or planet.

It highlights the musical act Odesza.

Bumper 4

This bumper’s design is based upon the concept of how molecules of a gas stick together, and move about rapidly to maintain the gaseous state. It creates a sense of floating, as the festival transports you to a different place.

It highlights the artist St. Vincent.

Snipe

The snipe is a simple overlay that appears in the lower third of festival footage. It is designed to showcase three upcoming acts in the lineup. This can be displayed on projected screens to inform festival goers.

Case Study

All design aspects and motion graphics were compiled into a concsise explainer video to showcase them as a series.
